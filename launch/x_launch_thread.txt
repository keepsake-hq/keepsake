1/
Every "AI memory" product stores your context on their servers. Readable by them. Locked to
one model. Gone if they pivot.

So I built the opposite: Keepsake — a long-term memory for LLMs that lives on YOUR machine,
encrypted, owned like a wallet. Open source. Here's how 🧵

2/
The problem isn't "remembering." It's custody.

Your notes, your plans, the stuff you tell an AI all day — that's some of your most personal
data. It shouldn't live in a SaaS account you can't read and can't truly delete.

3/
The insight: treat memory like a crypto wallet, not a login.

One 24-word seed derives every key. The whole store is a single SQLCipher-encrypted file on
your disk. No account, no server. Lose the seed = lose the data. That's the trade for real
ownership.

4/
How it works:
• On-device embeddings (Nomic/ONNX) → semantic recall, fully local
• Random per-cell key → `forget` destroys the KEY, so ciphertext is unrecoverable for real
• OpenAI-compatible proxy + MCP → works with any model, Claude, Cursor
• PQC sharing: X25519 + ML-KEM-768, ML-DSA-65 signatures

5/
Proof it's semantic + local: I typed "what should I get my friend?" and it recalled "buy a
birthday gift for Sarah" — no shared words, no internet.

Don't trust me (I'm anonymous): no telemetry, runs with Wi-Fi off, ~4,900 lines of Rust you
can read. [demo clip]

6/
The honest part most tools hide: zero-knowledge AT REST is real; zero-knowledge DURING CLOUD
INFERENCE is not. Anything you send a cloud model is plaintext to them.

So Keepsake defaults to your LOCAL model, and makes any cloud send an explicit, logged choice.

7/
It's Apache-2.0, ~4,900 LOC, 12 small crates, zero accounts, runs offline. macOS desktop app
(Tauri v2) + CLI.
